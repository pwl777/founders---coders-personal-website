Introduction

Welcome to the site, my name is Pierson Lippard. My career background, since Art College and Film School has been busy creating visual media. From Film & TV animation, to Games and Film Visual Effects. I'm a hard working enthusiast with plenty of drive and initiative.

In April 2020 I began my journey exploring the exciting world of other tech disciplines. From ethical hacking to self taught web development and have enjoyed every moment so far. I've had great fun creating over 100 DOM projects practising my Front End Developer knowledge. I've included a small selection of these interactive GitHub projects, for you to explore in the projects section below.

I love sports of all kinds, including Sumo, but my favorite to watch and play, is Baseball. I also enjoy learning Japanese and since last year I've really perfected my bread baking skills.

My Coding Journey

Being a Maya 3D artist, I started learning Python during the first lockdown. I love creating something, and had fun building my first webpage with Python & Django. But I really wanted to have full creative freedom over the visual style of my new page. so I concentrated on learning symantic HTML5 along with CSS3, and practiced this knowledge by creating a catalogue of fun DOM projects, which I've hosted on Github pages.

I think it was sometime in late August 2020, when I started to persue the prospects of a career change from visual effects artist to front end developer. So I expanded these DOM projects by exploring and practicing the amazing possibilities of what Javascript can allow you to create.

I really enjoy the empowered feeling of learning new skills and overcoming problems. But struggling constantly on your own can become unnecessarily frustrating and counter productive. So I started searching for a coding community that I can interact with and hopefully become an integral part of. Participating in the application process with Founders & Coders from early May 2021 has already been a great help with moving me forward to reaching my dreams. I am very grateful for this experience.

A Better Vision

It's amazing what positive outcomes can actually emerge out of an adverse situation. The pandemic lockdowns had given me an important moment, to stop and reflect on the recursive direction my life had been traveling in and to assess what kind of dreams and improvements in lifestyle that I want to achieve, for both myself and my family.

It was at the start of this reflective time, when my coding journey began. I was utterly surprised, when I discovered how much I really enjoyed learning to code. The more I explored the amazing possibilities of what can be created using code. Coupled with the potential multitude of job oportunities programming had to offer, the more excited I became about entering this new world and the future it could provide.

Programming has given me a fantastic vision of a much freeier present and future, with far more career opportunities in multiple locations, above any that working in the small niche world of visual effects can give to both my family and I. I also love the variety of visually diverse things I can build with coding. The endless possibilities are just so exciting. I am determind to work hard and master Javascript so I can reach my goals and help others in the community.

My Goals

My personal goal is to live an ecologically sustainable lifestyle within 5 years, becoming a front End Developer will give me the career flexibility to achieve this goal. One of my other goals is that I also want to help others learn essential programming skills, especially people who are aged 50+ to help them bridge the increasing technical skills casum. By this process I also hope to introduce more age diversity into the programming world.

The latest report from the Chartered Institute for IT, formally the BCS (British Computer Society), found that the level of representation of people aged 50 and above, working in the tech industry. Is way below the the level of representation considered normal in any other workforce. Web development is very porley represented, with only 10% of actual developers, aged 50+.

According to the latest government research the UK tech market has a skills shortage of 480,000 people. Of which, the representative percentage of people aged 50+, should be around 119,000 workers.

Going through my own process of upskilling, has given me a great insight into how I can better help others develop their knowledge of programming. It's a difficult but very rewarding process to go through. The future benefit to both the industry and the individuals who want to work in the sector would be emense.
